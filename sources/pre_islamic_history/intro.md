# Pre-Islamic Iranian History

## Overview

Pre-Islamic Iranian history spans from the earliest Iranian civilizations through the fall of the Sasanian Empire in 651 CE. This period encompasses the rise and fall of several major Iranian dynasties and the development of Zoroastrianism as a major world religion.

## Achaemenid Empire (550-330 BCE)

The **Achaemenid Empire** was founded by **Cyrus the Great** (Cyrus II) around 550 BCE and became the largest empire the ancient world had yet seen, stretching from the Indus Valley to Egypt and Anatolia.

### Rise of the Empire

**Cyrus the Great** established the Achaemenid dynasty after defeating the Median Empire, followed by conquests of Lydia and Babylon. His policy of religious tolerance, exemplified in the **Cyrus Cylinder** (c. 539 BCE), earned him respect throughout his empire.

### Administrative System

The Achaemenids developed an efficient administrative system:

- **Satrapies**: Provincial divisions governed by satraps (governors)
- **Royal Road**: An extensive communication network enabling rapid message delivery
- **Standardized currency**: The daric gold coin became widely accepted
- **Religious tolerance**: Allowed local customs and religions to continue

### Darius I and the Persepolis Inscriptions

**Darius I "the Great"** (r. 522-486 BCE) expanded the empire and initiated major building projects, including **Persepolis**, which served as the ceremonial capital. His inscriptions in Old Persian, Elamite, and Babylonian established the Achaemenid royal inscriptions tradition.

## Parthian/Seleucid Era (247 BCE - 224 CE)

### Seleucid Interregnum

After Alexander the Great's conquest of the Achaemenid Empire (330 BCE), his successors established the **Seleucid Empire**, which controlled much of the former Persian territories. However, Iranian peoples gradually regained independence:

- **Parthians** (Arsacid dynasty) established control in northeastern Iran around 247 BCE
- **Greco-Bactrian Kingdom** controlled Central Asian territories
- Various local dynasties reasserted Iranian identity

### Parthian Resurgence

The **Parthian Empire** under the **Arsacid dynasty** successfully challenged Seleucid power and established a new Iranian empire:

- **Mithridates I** (r. c. 171-138 BCE) expanded Parthian control westward
- **Mithridates II** (r. c. 124-88 BCE) established Parthia as Rome's primary eastern rival
- Military system based on **cataphracts** (heavily armored cavalry) and horse archers

### Parthian Administrative System

The Parthians adopted a more feudal system than the Achaemenids:

- Vassal kings and local rulers maintained significant autonomy
- Synthesis of Iranian and Hellenistic administrative practices
- Promotion of Zoroastrianism alongside local religions

## Sasanian Empire (224-651 CE)

### Rise of the Sasanians

The **Sasanian Empire** was founded by **Ardashir I** (r. 224-242 CE) after defeating the last Parthian king, **Artabanus IV**. The Sasanians claimed descent from the Achaemenids and sought to restore the glory of ancient Iran.

### State and Religion

The Sasanians established **Zoroastrianism** as the state religion, creating the first truly theocratic empire in Iranian history:

- Codification of Zoroastrian texts, including the **Avesta**
- Centralized religious hierarchy under the **Magi**
- Integration of religious and political authority
- Conflict with Christian communities, particularly after Constantine's conversion

### Sasanian Military and Society

Sasanian society was highly stratified:

- **Shahanshah** (King of Kings) at the apex
- Military nobility and **wuzurgan** (higher nobility)
- Professional classes and merchant communities
- Peasantry and artisans
- Slaves

The military was organized around the four **aswaran** (cavalry divisions) and included specialized units like:
- Heavy cavalry (cataphracts)
- War elephants
- Siege engineers
- Elite royal guard

### Cultural Achievements

The Sasanian period saw significant cultural achievements:

- Revival of Persian literature and historical writing
- Development of Sasanian art and architecture
- Medical and astronomical advances
- The **House of Wisdom** tradition that would later influence Islamic civilization

## Key Chronological Points

- c. 550 BCE: Cyrus the Great founds Achaemenid Empire
- 522 BCE: Darius I begins reign
- 330 BCE: Alexander conquers Achaemenid Empire
- 247 BCE: Parthian independence begins
- 224 CE: Ardashir I founds Sasanian Empire
- 651 CE: Fall of Sasanian Empire to Arab conquest

## Teaching Notes

This section covered the major periods of pre-Islamic Iranian history. Understanding the chronological sequence and the transitions between dynasties is crucial. The administrative systems of each period show evolution in Iranian statecraft, from the Achaemenid centralized satrapy system to the more feudal Parthian structure to the theocratic Sasanian state. These historical foundations are essential for understanding later Islamic period developments in Iran.
